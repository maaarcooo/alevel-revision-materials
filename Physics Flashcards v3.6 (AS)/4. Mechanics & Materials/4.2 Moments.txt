What is a moment? | The turning effect of a force
The turning effect of a force — what term is this? | Moment
What is the equation for the moment of a force? | Moment = Force x perpendicular distance from the pivot
What is the SI unit of moment? | Newton metre (N m)
How is the moment calculated when the force is not perpendicular to the distance from the pivot? | Take the component of the distance perpendicular to the force: Moment = F x d cos(theta)
Explain why a door handle is placed far from the hinge | Maximises the perpendicular distance from the pivot, increasing the moment for a given force and making the door easier to open
State the principle of moments | For a system to be in equilibrium, the sum of clockwise moments about a point must equal the sum of anticlockwise moments about the same point
The sum of clockwise moments equals the sum of anticlockwise moments about the same point — what principle is this? | The principle of moments
What is a couple? | A pair of equal and opposite coplanar forces that produces rotation only
A pair of equal and opposite coplanar forces that produces rotation only — what is this called? | A couple
What three properties must the forces in a couple have? | Equal in magnitude, opposite in direction, and perpendicular to the distance between them
Why does a couple produce no linear acceleration? | The resultant force is zero (equal and opposite forces cancel), so by F = ma there is no acceleration
What is the equation for the moment of a couple? | Moment = Force x perpendicular distance between the lines of action of the forces
How does the moment of a couple differ from the moment of a single force? | The moment of a couple does not depend on a pivot, unlike the moment of a single force
What is the centre of mass of an object? | The point at which the weight of the object may be considered to act
The point at which the weight of an object may be considered to act — what is this called? | Centre of mass
Where is the centre of mass of a symmetrical object with uniform density? | At the point of symmetry
When is an object stable in terms of its centre of mass? | When its centre of mass lies above its base
What combination of properties makes an object most stable? | A wide base and a low centre of mass
When are the centre of gravity and centre of mass of an object identical? | In a uniform gravitational field
How does the centre of mass differ from the centre of gravity? | Centre of mass does not depend on the gravitational field, but centre of gravity does (since weight = mg depends on g)
Where can the centre of mass of a body lie? | Inside or outside the body, since it is a hypothetical point
