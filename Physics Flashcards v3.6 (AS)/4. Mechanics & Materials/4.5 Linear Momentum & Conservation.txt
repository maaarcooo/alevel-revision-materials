Define linear momentum | The product of mass and velocity (p = mv)
The product of mass and velocity — what quantity is this? | Linear momentum
What are the SI units of momentum? | kg m s⁻¹
What type of physical quantity is momentum? | A vector, with both magnitude and direction
In momentum problems, what sign is conventionally assigned to the initial direction of motion? | Positive
If an object reverses direction after a collision, what happens to the sign of its velocity and momentum? | They become negative (opposite to the chosen positive direction)
State the principle of conservation of linear momentum | The total momentum before a collision equals the total momentum after, provided no external resultant force acts
The total momentum before a collision equals the total momentum after, provided no external resultant force acts — what principle is this? | The principle of conservation of linear momentum
What is the conservation of momentum equation for two objects A and B? | p_Ai + p_Bi = p_Af + p_Bf
What are external forces in a momentum system? | Forces that act on the system from outside, e.g. friction and weight
What are internal forces in a momentum system? | Forces exchanged by particles within the system, e.g. tension in a string
What terms describe a system with no external forces acting? | Closed or isolated
Express Newton's second law in terms of momentum | F = Δp/Δt (force equals the rate of change of momentum)
What is the equation for change in momentum? | Δp = p_final − p_initial = mv − mu
Define impulse | The change in momentum of an object caused by a force acting over a short time (Impulse = FΔt)
The change in momentum of an object caused by a force acting over a short time — what quantity is this? | Impulse
What are the units of impulse? | Newton seconds (N s)
What type of physical quantity is impulse? | A vector, in the direction of the resultant force
The impulse equations (FΔt = Δp) apply only when what condition is met regarding force? | The force F must be constant
On a force-time graph, what does the area under the curve represent? | Impulse (change in momentum)
How is impulse found from a curved force-time graph? | By counting the squares under the curve
How is impulse found from a force-time graph made of straight lines? | Split into sections (triangles, rectangles, trapeziums) and sum their areas
For a given change in momentum, what happens to the impact force if the contact time is increased? | The force decreases, since F = Δp/Δt
Explain why a cricket fielder moves their hands backwards when catching a ball | Increases the time over which the ball's momentum changes, reducing the force on their hands
Explain why following through when kicking a football gives the ball a higher velocity | Longer contact time increases the impulse transferred to the ball, giving it greater momentum
Explain why hailstones exert more force on an umbrella than raindrops | Hailstones bounce back (larger change in momentum), while raindrops splatter (smaller change), so hailstones deliver greater impulse
How does packaging (e.g. bubble wrap) reduce damage to fragile items in transit? | It cushions items by increasing the time over which forces act, reducing the impact force
What defines an elastic collision? | Kinetic energy is conserved (as well as momentum)
What defines an inelastic collision? | Kinetic energy is not conserved (though momentum is still conserved)
How do elastic and inelastic collisions differ? | Both conserve momentum, but only elastic collisions also conserve kinetic energy
How do you determine whether a collision is elastic or inelastic? | Calculate total kinetic energy before and after using Ek = ½mv² and compare
What is the equation for kinetic energy? | Ek = ½mv²
When two objects stick together after a collision, how is the combined mass treated in calculations? | As a single object with mass equal to m_A + m_B
In the context of momentum, what is an explosion? | An event where objects move apart from rest (e.g. gun recoil, nuclear decay), with total momentum conserved at zero
A swimmer dives forward from a stationary boat. Explain why the boat moves backwards | Total initial momentum is zero, so by conservation of momentum the boat gains momentum equal and opposite to the swimmer's
How do vehicle safety features (crumple zones, seat belts, airbags) reduce injury? | They increase the contact time over which the passenger's momentum changes, reducing the force experienced (F = Δp/Δt)
What specific mechanism allows seat belts to increase deceleration time? | They are designed to stretch slightly on impact
Where are crumple zones located on a vehicle, and how do they deform? | At the front and rear, designed to crush in a controlled way during a collision
On a force-time graph, how does wearing a seat belt change the curve for the same impulse? | Lower peak force and wider time base, with the same area under the graph
According to Newton's Third Law, how do the forces between two colliding objects compare? | Equal in magnitude and opposite in direction
What are the base SI units of the newton? | kg m s⁻²
