What is the Young Modulus? | The ratio of tensile stress to tensile strain for a material. It indicates the stiffness of the material
The ratio of tensile stress to tensile strain — what quantity is this? | The Young Modulus
State the equation for the Young Modulus in terms of F, L, A, and ΔL | E = FL / (AΔL)
What are the units of the Young Modulus? | Pascals (Pa)
Explain why the Young Modulus has the same units as stress | Strain is dimensionless (a ratio of two lengths), so dividing stress by strain gives units of Pa
What is the typical order of magnitude of the Young Modulus for metals? | Gigapascals (GPa)
How is the Young Modulus determined from a stress-strain graph? | It equals the gradient of the linear region (where Hooke's Law is obeyed)
What does the area under the linear region of a stress-strain graph represent? | Energy stored per unit volume of the material
What is the aim of the Young Modulus required practical? | To measure the Young Modulus of a metal in the form of a wire
In the Young Modulus required practical, what is the independent variable? | Force (load) applied to the wire
In the Young Modulus required practical, what is the dependent variable? | Extension of the wire
State the control variables in the Young Modulus required practical | Original length of wire, thickness of wire, and the metal used
How is the diameter of the wire measured in the Young Modulus practical? | Using a micrometer screw gauge (or digital callipers), taking at least 3 readings at different positions and averaging
What is the resolution of a micrometer screw gauge? | 0.001 mm
What is the resolution of a metre ruler? | 1 mm
What is the purpose of the tape marker in the Young Modulus practical? | To mark a reference point on the wire so extension can be measured against the ruler
What is the purpose of the pulley in the Young Modulus practical? | To allow the mass to hang vertically, with less friction than the edge of the table
What equation gives the cross-sectional area of the wire from its diameter? | A = πd² / 4
What graph is plotted to determine the Young Modulus in the required practical? | Force (load) on the y-axis against extension on the x-axis
How is the Young Modulus calculated from the force-extension graph? | Young Modulus = gradient of the graph multiplied by L / A, where L is original length and A is cross-sectional area
What does the gradient of the force-extension graph equal in the Young Modulus practical? | (Young Modulus multiplied by A) / L
Explain how extending the wire past its elastic limit affects the Young Modulus experiment | The wire becomes permanently deformed, so it no longer returns to its original length and subsequent readings are invalid
How is permanent deformation of the wire avoided during the Young Modulus practical? | Remove the load after each reading and check the wire returns to its original length before adding the next load
What is a source of random error in the Young Modulus practical? | Parallax error from reading the position of the marker on the ruler
How are random errors reduced in the Young Modulus practical? | Repeat the experiment for all loads and calculate an average extension
Why should the diameter be measured at several positions along the wire? | To reduce the uncertainty in the cross-sectional area, since the wire may not be perfectly uniform
How can the precision of extension measurements be improved in the Young Modulus practical? | Use a vernier scale instead of a standard metre ruler
State the safety precautions for the Young Modulus practical | Wear safety goggles in case the wire snaps. Place a cushion or soft surface below the mass hanger in case it falls
Why does using a longer wire improve the Young Modulus experiment? | A longer wire produces a larger extension for the same load, reducing the percentage uncertainty in extension measurements
