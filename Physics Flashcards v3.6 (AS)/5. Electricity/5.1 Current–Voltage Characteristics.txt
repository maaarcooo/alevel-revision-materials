Define electric current | The rate of flow of electric charge
The rate of flow of electric charge — what quantity is this? | Electric current
What is the equation for electric current in terms of charge and time? | I = ΔQ/Δt
What is the unit of electric current? | Ampere (A)
What is 1 ampere equivalent to in terms of charge and time? | 1 coulomb of charge flowing per second (1 A = 1 C s⁻¹)
What two things does a simple electrical circuit require? | A conductor through which current can flow, and a source of potential difference
What is conventional current? | The flow of positive charge, from the positive terminal to the negative terminal of a cell
The flow of positive charge from positive to negative terminal — what term describes this? | Conventional current
In which direction do electrons flow in a circuit relative to conventional current? | Opposite direction: electrons flow from the negative terminal to the positive terminal
How must an ammeter be connected in a circuit? | In series with the component being measured
Define potential difference | The electrical work done per unit charge flowing between two points
The electrical work done per unit charge flowing between two points — what quantity is this? | Potential difference
What is the equation for potential difference in terms of work done and charge? | V = W/Q
What is the unit of potential difference? | Volt (V)
What is 1 volt equivalent to in terms of energy and charge? | The transfer of 1 joule of energy per coulomb of charge (1 V = 1 J C⁻¹)
What creates a potential difference in a simple cell? | The separation of charge: one terminal has excess positive charge and the other has excess negative charge
What happens to the energy of electrons as they flow through a cell? | They gain energy (the cell does work on them)
What happens to the energy of electrons as they flow through circuit components? | They lose energy, transferring it to the components as heat, light, etc.
How must a voltmeter be connected in a circuit? | In parallel with the component being measured
How does potential difference differ from energy? | Potential difference is energy per unit charge (V = W/Q), not total energy. Two batteries with the same p.d. can store different amounts of energy
Define resistance | The opposition of a component to the flow of electric current through it
The opposition of a component to the flow of electric current through it — what quantity is this? | Resistance
What is the equation relating resistance, potential difference, and current? | R = V/I (equivalently V = IR)
What is the unit of resistance? | Ohm (Ω)
For a given potential difference, how does increasing the resistance affect the current? | The current decreases
Why are connecting wires typically made from copper? | Copper has low electrical resistance, making it a good conductor
In exam questions, what value is assumed for the resistance of connecting wires? | Zero
State Ohm's law | For a conductor at constant temperature, the current through it is proportional to the potential difference across it
For a conductor at constant temperature, the current through it is proportional to the potential difference across it — what law is this? | Ohm's law
What physical condition must remain constant for a conductor to obey Ohm's law? | Temperature (constant temperature ensures constant resistance)
How can you tell from an I-V graph whether a component obeys Ohm's law? | The graph is a straight line through the origin
Give an example of a component that obeys Ohm's law | A fixed resistor (or metallic conductor) at constant temperature
On an I-V graph (current vs potential difference), what does the gradient represent? | 1/R (the reciprocal of resistance)
In the circuit used to investigate I-V characteristics, how is the potential difference across the component varied? | By adjusting a variable resistor connected in series with the component
Describe the I-V graph for a semiconductor diode | Near-zero current in reverse bias, then a sharp increase in current beyond the threshold voltage in forward bias
What is forward bias in a semiconductor diode? | When current flows in the direction of the diode's arrowhead symbol, allowing current to pass
What is reverse bias in a semiconductor diode? | When the diode is connected in the opposite direction; it does not conduct
Describe the I-V graph for a filament lamp | An S-shaped curve: approximately linear near the origin, then the gradient decreases at higher potential differences in both directions
Explain why the current through a filament lamp increases at a slower rate as potential difference increases | As current increases, the filament temperature rises. Since the filament is a metal, higher temperature increases its resistance, opposing further current flow
The filament lamp obeys Ohm's law for [...] | small voltages (where the I-V graph is approximately linear)
A charge of 4 C passes through a circuit in 500 s. What is the current? | I = ΔQ/Δt = 4/500 = 8 x 10⁻³ A = 8 mA
