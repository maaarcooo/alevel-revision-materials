Define electromotive force (e.m.f.) | The amount of chemical energy converted to electrical energy per coulomb of charge passing through a power supply
The amount of chemical energy converted to electrical energy per coulomb of charge passing through a power supply — what term is this? | Electromotive force (e.m.f.)
What symbol represents e.m.f.? | ε (Greek letter epsilon)
What is the unit of e.m.f.? | Volts (V)
What is the equation for e.m.f. in terms of energy and charge? | ε = E/Q, where E is energy transformed from other forms to electrical and Q is charge
When is the e.m.f. of a cell equal to the potential difference across its terminals? | When no current is flowing (open circuit)
How can e.m.f. be measured experimentally? | Connect a high-resistance voltmeter in parallel with the cell in an open circuit
What is terminal potential difference? | The potential difference across the terminals of a cell
Why is the terminal p.d. always lower than the e.m.f. when current flows? | Some energy per unit charge is dissipated overcoming the internal resistance of the cell
Define lost volts | The work done per unit charge to overcome the internal resistance inside the battery when current flows
The work done per unit charge to overcome the internal resistance inside the battery when current flows — what term is this? | Lost volts
What is the equation for lost volts? | v = Ir, where I is current and r is internal resistance
How are e.m.f., terminal p.d., and lost volts related by conservation of energy? | ε = V + v (e.m.f. equals terminal p.d. plus lost volts)
Define internal resistance | The resistance of the materials within the battery
The resistance of the materials within the battery — what term is this? | Internal resistance
Why does a cell become warm when current flows through it for a period of time? | Internal resistance causes circulating charge to dissipate electrical energy as heat within the power supply
What is the e.m.f. equation in terms of current, load resistance, and internal resistance? | ε = I(R + r), where I is current, R is load resistance, and r is internal resistance
How is a real cell modelled in circuit analysis? | As a source of e.m.f. with an internal resistance connected in series
Besides energy transferred per unit charge, how else can e.m.f. be described? | The total or maximum voltage available to the circuit
If a current of 0.5 A flows through a cell with internal resistance 2 Ω, what are the lost volts? | v = Ir = 0.5 x 2 = 1 V
If an exam question states a battery has "negligible internal resistance", what does this mean for calculations? | The e.m.f. equals the terminal p.d., and no internal resistance calculations are needed
What is the aim of the required practical for investigating e.m.f. and internal resistance? | To investigate the relationship between e.m.f. and internal resistance by measuring the variation of current and voltage using a variable resistor
In the e.m.f. and internal resistance practical, what is the independent variable? | Resistance of the variable resistor, R
In the e.m.f. and internal resistance practical, what are the dependent variables? | Voltage, V (V) and current, I (A)
In the e.m.f. and internal resistance practical, what are the control variables? | E.m.f. of the cell and internal resistance of the cell
What equation is used for the straight-line graph in the e.m.f. and internal resistance practical? | V = -rI + ε, which has the form y = mx + c
On a V against I graph for the e.m.f. and internal resistance practical, what does the gradient represent? | The negative of the internal resistance (-r)
On a V against I graph for the e.m.f. and internal resistance practical, what does the y-intercept represent? | The e.m.f. (ε)
Why must the switch be opened between readings in the e.m.f. and internal resistance practical? | To prevent the internal resistance of the battery from changing during the experiment (avoids systematic error from the cell running down)
How are random errors reduced in the e.m.f. and internal resistance practical? | Use fairly new cells, wait for readings to stabilise before recording, and take at least 3 repeat readings to calculate a mean
How many sets of V and I readings should be taken in the e.m.f. and internal resistance practical? | At least 8 to 10 readings across the whole range of the variable resistor
State two safety precautions for the e.m.f. and internal resistance practical | Switch off the power supply immediately if burning is smelled, and keep liquids away from the electrical equipment
