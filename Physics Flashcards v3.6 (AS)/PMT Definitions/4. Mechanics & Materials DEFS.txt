What is elastic behaviour? | The material returns to its original shape when the deforming forces are removed, with no permanent deformation
A material that returns to its original shape after the deforming forces are removed exhibits [...] behaviour | elastic
What is plastic behaviour? | The material does not return to its original shape when the deforming forces are removed, resulting in permanent deformation
A material that is permanently deformed after the deforming forces are removed exhibits [...] behaviour | plastic
How does elastic behaviour differ from plastic behaviour? | Elastic: object returns to original shape when force is removed. Plastic: object is permanently deformed
Define elastic collision | A collision in which the total kinetic energy of the system is conserved
A collision in which total kinetic energy is conserved — what type of collision is this? | Elastic collision
Define inelastic collision | A collision in which the total kinetic energy of the system is not conserved
A collision in which total kinetic energy is not conserved — what type of collision is this? | Inelastic collision
How do elastic and inelastic collisions differ? | Elastic: total kinetic energy is conserved. Inelastic: total kinetic energy is not conserved. Both conserve momentum
Define elastic limit | The force beyond which a material no longer deforms elastically and instead deforms plastically

What is a vector quantity? | A quantity that has both magnitude and direction (e.g. velocity, displacement, acceleration)
