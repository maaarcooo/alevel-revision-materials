What does the Young modulus measure? | The ability of a material to withstand changes in length with an added load
The ability of a material to withstand changes in length under load — what property is this? | The Young modulus
What information does the Young modulus give about a material? | Its stiffness
Why is the Young modulus useful for engineers? | To ensure materials can withstand sufficient forces
How is the Young modulus defined? | The ratio of tensile stress to tensile strain
The ratio of tensile stress to tensile strain defines which quantity? | The Young modulus
Write the equation for Young modulus in terms of stress and strain | Young Modulus = Tensile stress / Tensile strain
Write the equation for Young modulus in terms of F, L, A, and ΔL | Young Modulus = FL / AΔL
In the Young modulus equation, what does F represent? | Force (N)
In the Young modulus equation, what does L represent? | Original length (m)
In the Young modulus equation, what does A represent? | Cross-sectional area (m²)
In the Young modulus equation, what does ΔL represent? | Extension (m)
Why is the unit of Young modulus pascals (Pa)? | Because strain is dimensionless, so the unit comes from stress alone
What are the units of the Young modulus? | Pascals (Pa)
What order of magnitude is the Young modulus typically expressed in? | GPa (gigapascals)
