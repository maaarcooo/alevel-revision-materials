What are SI Base Units? | The internationally standardised system of measurement units from which all other units can be derived, used officially in almost every country
The internationally standardised system of units from which all other units are derived — what is this called? | SI Base Units
How many SI base units are there in total? | Seven (six are required for AQA A-Level Physics)
What is the SI base unit of mass? | Kilogram (kg)
What is the SI base unit of length? | Metre (m)
What is the SI base unit of time? | Second (s)
What is the SI base unit of electric current? | Ampere (A)
What is the SI base unit of temperature? | Kelvin (K)
What is the SI base unit of amount of substance? | Mole (mol)

What are derived units? | Units that are constructed from combinations of the seven SI base units
Units constructed from combinations of SI base units are called what? | Derived units
How do you deduce the SI base units of a derived unit? | Use the definition (equation) of the quantity and substitute in base units
What is the Newton (N) in SI base units? | kg m s⁻²
The SI base units kg m s⁻² correspond to which derived unit? | Newton (N)
Which equation is used to derive the base units of the Newton? | Force = mass × acceleration (F = ma)
What is the Joule (J) in SI base units? | kg m² s⁻²
The SI base units kg m² s⁻² correspond to which derived unit? | Joule (J)
Which equation is used to derive the base units of the Joule? | Energy = ½ × mass × velocity² (Ek = ½mv²)
What is the Pascal (Pa) in SI base units? | kg m⁻¹ s⁻²
The SI base units kg m⁻¹ s⁻² correspond to which derived unit? | Pascal (Pa)
Which equation is used to derive the base units of the Pascal? | Pressure = force ÷ area (P = F/A)
How does the derivation of the Pascal build on the Newton? | Pa = N ÷ m² = (kg m s⁻²) ÷ m² = kg m⁻¹ s⁻²

What is an electronvolt (eV)? | The energy transferred when one electron moves through a potential difference of 1 V, equal to 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ J
A unit of energy equal to 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ J is called what? | One electronvolt (1 eV)
From which equation is the electronvolt derived? | W = qV (work done = charge × potential difference)
How is 1 eV calculated in Joules? | 1 eV = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C × 1 V = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ J
How do you convert from Joules to electronvolts? | Divide by 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹
How do you convert from electronvolts to Joules? | Multiply by 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹
How many Joules is 1 kW h equal to? | 3.6 × 10⁶ J (3.6 MJ)
How do you convert from Joules to kilowatt-hours? | Divide by 3.6 × 10⁶
How do you convert from kilowatt-hours to Joules? | Multiply by 3.6 × 10⁶
Explain the steps to derive that 1 kW h = 3.6 × 10⁶ J | 1 kW h = 1000 W × 3600 s = 3 600 000 W s = 3 600 000 J, since 1 W = 1 J s⁻¹
What is an order of magnitude? | The power of ten of a number when expressed in scientific notation, used to describe the approximate scale of a quantity
The power of ten that describes the approximate scale of a quantity is called what? | An order of magnitude
What is the order of magnitude of 3 × 10⁸? | 10⁸
What is the order of magnitude of 6 × 10⁸? | 10⁹ (rounded up because 6 > 5)
Explain how rounding rules apply to orders of magnitude | If the coefficient is less than 5 (e.g. 3 × 10⁸), the order of magnitude is the power itself (10⁸). If the coefficient is 5 or greater (e.g. 6 × 10⁸), the order of magnitude rounds up to the next power (10⁹)
A quantity is described as "one order of magnitude larger" than another. What does this mean? | It is approximately 10 times larger
A quantity is described as "two orders of magnitude larger" than another. What does this mean? | It is approximately 100 times (10²) larger
Why is scientific notation useful in physics? | Physical quantities span a huge range of values, so scientific notation avoids writing many zeros and makes comparison of scales easier

How does the Newton differ from the Pascal in SI base units? | The Newton is kg m s⁻² (force), while the Pascal is kg m⁻¹ s⁻² (pressure). They differ because Pa = N/m², so dividing by m² changes m to m⁻¹
How does the Joule differ from the Newton in SI base units? | The Joule is kg m² s⁻² (energy) while the Newton is kg m s⁻² (force). The Joule has an extra factor of metres because energy = force × distance
How do electronvolts (eV) and kilowatt-hours (kW h) compare as energy units? | Both are non-SI energy units. 1 eV = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ J (used at atomic/particle scale), while 1 kW h = 3.6 × 10⁶ J (used for domestic/industrial energy consumption). They differ by about 25 orders of magnitude
