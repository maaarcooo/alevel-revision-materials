What is the Young modulus? | A measure of the ability of a material to withstand changes in length with an added load
A measure of a material's ability to resist changes in length when a load is applied — what quantity is this? | The Young modulus
What property of a material does the Young modulus give information about? | Stiffness
Why is the Young modulus useful for engineers? | It tells them whether the materials they are using can withstand sufficient forces without deforming excessively
How is the Young modulus defined in terms of stress and strain? | The ratio of tensile stress to tensile strain
The Young modulus is the ratio of tensile stress to tensile strain — write this as an equation | Young modulus = tensile stress / tensile strain = FL / (A x deltaL)
What is the equation for the Young modulus in terms of F, L, A, and deltaL? | E = FL / (A x deltaL)
In the Young modulus equation E = FL / (A x deltaL), what does F represent? | Force applied (N)
In the Young modulus equation E = FL / (A x deltaL), what does L represent? | Original length of the material (m)
In the Young modulus equation E = FL / (A x deltaL), what does A represent? | Cross-sectional area (m^2)
In the Young modulus equation E = FL / (A x deltaL), what does deltaL represent? | Extension (m)
What are the units of the Young modulus? | Pascals (Pa)
Why are the units of the Young modulus the same as those of stress? | Because strain is dimensionless (it has no units), so the units of stress/strain are just the units of stress, which are Pa
The Young modulus of a material is typically a very large number — what order of magnitude is common? | Gigapascals (GPa)
What is the Young modulus of aluminium? | 70 GPa
What is the Young modulus of copper? | 117 GPa
What is the Young modulus of brass? | 120 GPa
What is the Young modulus of iron? | 170 GPa
What is the Young modulus of nickel? | 210 GPa
What is the Young modulus of steel? | 110 GPa
Which has a higher Young modulus: copper or steel? | Copper (117 GPa vs 110 GPa)
Which material in the common metals table has the highest Young modulus? | Nickel (210 GPa)
How is the Young modulus found from a stress-strain graph? | It equals the gradient of the linear (straight-line) region of the graph
On a stress-strain graph, in which region does the gradient equal the Young modulus? | The linear region where Hooke's law is obeyed
What does the area under the linear region of a stress-strain graph represent? | The energy stored per unit volume of the material
The energy stored per unit volume of a material can be found from what feature of a stress-strain graph? | The area under the graph in the linear (Hooke's law) region
How does the Young modulus differ from the spring constant (stiffness constant k)? | The Young modulus is a material property (independent of dimensions), while the spring constant depends on the object's length and cross-sectional area
Explain why a longer wire of the same material has a smaller spring constant but the same Young modulus | A longer wire extends more for the same force (lower k), but the Young modulus accounts for original length and cross-sectional area, so it depends only on the material, not dimensions
What is a useful mnemonic for remembering whether stress or strain is on top in the Young modulus equation? | "When you're stressed, you show the strain" — stress divided by strain
What is the aim of the Young modulus required practical? | To measure the Young modulus of a metal in the form of a wire
What is the independent variable in the Young modulus experiment? | Force (or load) applied to the wire (N)
What is the dependent variable in the Young modulus experiment? | Extension of the wire (m)
Name three control variables in the Young modulus experiment | Original length of wire, thickness of wire, and the metal used for the wire
What is used to measure the diameter of the wire in the Young modulus experiment? | A micrometer screw gauge (or digital callipers)
Why must you take at least 3 readings of the wire diameter and average them? | To reduce uncertainty on the cross-sectional area, since the wire may not be perfectly uniform
What is the resolution of a metre ruler? | 1 mm
What is the resolution of a micrometer? | 0.001 mm
What is the purpose of the pulley in the Young modulus experiment? | To allow the mass to hang vertically and to reduce friction compared to having the wire run over the edge of the table
What is the purpose of the tape marker on the wire? | To accurately measure the extension of the wire with the applied load — it should touch the ruler
Why are wooden blocks used in the Young modulus experiment? | To clamp the wire and keep it taut and straight
What mass increments are typically added in the Young modulus experiment? | 100 g masses, added one at a time (at least 5 to 10 times)
In the Young modulus experiment, how do you convert mass (in grams) to load (in newtons)? | Multiply the mass in kg by g (9.81 N/kg)
What equation is used to calculate the cross-sectional area of the wire from its diameter? | A = pi x d^2 / 4
What graph is plotted in the Young modulus experiment? | Load (force) on the y-axis against extension on the x-axis
How do you obtain the Young modulus from a force-extension graph? | Young modulus = gradient of the graph x (L / A), where L is the original length and A is the cross-sectional area
In the rearranged equation F = (Young modulus x A / L) x deltaL, what does the gradient of a force-extension graph represent? | (Young modulus x A) / L
After finding the gradient of a force-extension graph, how do you calculate the Young modulus? | Young modulus = gradient x (L / A)
Name a systematic error in the Young modulus experiment and how to reduce it | Reading extension without a vernier scale — use a vernier scale for more precise readings
What happens if the wire is extended past its elastic limit during the experiment? | The wire is permanently deformed and will not return to its original length, invalidating subsequent measurements
How can you check that the wire has not been permanently deformed during the experiment? | Remove the load and check that the wire returns to its original length before taking new readings
Name a source of random error in the Young modulus experiment | Parallax error from reading the marker position on the ruler
How are random errors reduced in the Young modulus experiment? | By repeating the experiment for all loads and finding an average extension
Why should the diameter of the wire be measured in several places? | To reduce uncertainty on the cross-sectional area, since the wire may vary in thickness along its length
State two safety precautions for the Young modulus experiment | Wear safety goggles in case the wire snaps, and place a cushion or soft surface below the mass hanger in case it falls
Why should safety goggles be worn during the Young modulus experiment? | The wire is under tension and could snap, posing a risk to the eyes
Why is a cushion placed below the mass hanger? | To prevent damage or injury if the masses fall off during the experiment
How does a stress-strain graph differ from a force-extension graph in what it tells you? | A stress-strain graph gives material properties (gradient = Young modulus, independent of dimensions), while a force-extension graph gives object properties (gradient = spring constant, depends on length and area)
Why is a long wire preferred in the Young modulus experiment? | A longer wire produces a larger extension for the same stress, making the extension easier to measure accurately and reducing percentage uncertainty
